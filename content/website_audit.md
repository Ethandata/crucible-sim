## 1. Current Headline & Title

- **Page Title:** "Crucible — Economic Autonomy Standard"
- **Meta Description:** "The standard for deciding how much economic autonomy an AI agent can safely have. Stress-test agents under scarcity, delegation, and cost pressure."
- **H1 (Hero):** "Your agent is on trial."
- **Hero Eyebrow:** "Pre-Deployment Agent Trial"

---

## 2. SOC2-for-Agents / Reliability Standard Value Prop — Gap Analysis

**What the site currently communicates:**
- Framing: "pre-deployment stress test" and "economic autonomy standard"
- Strong technical credibility: D1-D9 dimensions, Phi score, JSON-T signed traces, hidden eval sets
- Enterprise CTA exists (/pro) — "Request Enterprise Access"
- Replay/certification language: "Certified Report," "Signed traces," "Trace integrity and signature validation"

**What is MISSING vs. the SOC2-for-agents / reliability standard positioning:**
- No explicit "certification standard" or "compliance standard" language on the homepage
- The page title uses "Economic Autonomy Standard" — this is narrow; doesn't signal the broader reliability/certification story
- No "third-party" or "independent standard" framing — which is the core enterprise trust signal
- No mention of who the buyers are (platform teams, AI governance leads, CTOs deploying agents to production)
- The hero talks about WHAT (stress test) but not WHY it matters to enterprise (liability, compliance, customer trust)
- No social proof / logos / early adopters yet (understandable at this stage)
- The CTA "Run the Benchmark" goes to GitHub — appropriate for developers but the enterprise buyer needs a different path
- "Economic Autonomy Standard" framing conflicts slightly with the broader "reliability for enterprise AI agents" positioning Sales is using in outreach

---

## 3. Gaps vs. Sales-001 Outreach Templates

Sales outreach angle: "AI agents are being deployed into critical enterprise workflows. There is no independent standard for whether they actually work."

**Alignment gaps:**
- Website doesn't use "independent standard" language — enterprise buyers coming from outreach won't immediately see the landing they expect
- No "enterprise workflow reliability" framing on homepage — the site leans more toward dev-tool/benchmark than enterprise compliance tool
- The enterprise section (#enterprise / /pro) is minimal — needs a stronger value prop for procurement-minded buyers
- No explainer of what a "certified" agent means in business terms (risk reduction, audit trail, deployment gate)

---

## 4. Specific Copy Recommendations

### Headline:
**Current:** "Your agent is on trial."  
**Recommendation:** Keep — it's strong, memorable, and provocative. Add a sub-eyebrow for enterprise context.

### Page Title / Meta:
**Current:** "Crucible — Economic Autonomy Standard"  
**Recommended:** "Crucible — The Reliability Standard for Enterprise AI Agents"  
Rationale: Aligns with Sales positioning and broader ICP framing.

### Meta Description:
**Recommended:** "Crucible is the independent benchmark and certification standard for enterprise AI agent reliability. Stress-test agents before they touch production workflows."

### Hero Lead (addition):
Consider adding after the current hero lead: *"Used by engineering teams to gate deployment before agents touch customer data, financial workflows, or critical operations."*

### New Section (between hero and proof-grid):
**"The Independent Standard"** — short section explaining:  
- Why self-testing isn't enough  
- What a Crucible certification means  
- Who trusts the score (internal teams, procurement, auditors)

### Enterprise Section (/pro):
**Current CTA:** "Before an agent gets budget, prove it survives the trial."  
**Recommended addition:** "Crucible gives enterprise teams a defensible, auditable answer to: *Is this agent safe to deploy?*"

---

## 5. Overall Assessment

**Strengths:** Technically credible, visually distinctive, strong benchmark mechanics (D1-D9, Phi, JSON-T), good developer hook.

**Primary Gap:** The "enterprise reliability standard / SOC2-equivalent for agents" value prop is implied but not stated. A buyer from the Sales outreach sequence arrives and sees a cool benchmark tool — not an enterprise compliance solution. This needs to be bridged with 1-2 sentences on the homepage and a stronger /pro page.

**Priority fix:** Page title + meta description + one added paragraph in the enterprise CTA section. Low effort, high alignment gain.
